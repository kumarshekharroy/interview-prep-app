# Salary Negotiation

This file prepares compensation conversations for senior full-stack, senior .NET backend, Azure-focused, and lead engineer roles.

## Negotiation Principles

1. Research before giving numbers.
2. Discuss total compensation, not only base salary.
3. Understand role level and scope before anchoring tightly.
4. Ask for written offer details.
5. Do not accept under pressure.
6. Use a decision matrix.
7. Stay calm and professional.

## Compensation Components

| Component | Notes |
| --- | --- |
| Base salary | fixed cash compensation |
| Annual bonus | target and actual payout history matter |
| Equity/RSUs | vesting schedule, refreshers, liquidity |
| Joining bonus | useful for transition or competing offers |
| Benefits | health, retirement, insurance, wellness |
| Remote/hybrid policy | affects real value and quality of life |
| Role level | senior/lead mapping affects future growth |
| Learning/growth | architecture, cloud, ownership, mentoring |
| Manager/team quality | major long-term factor |

## Expected Salary - Recruiter Screen

Use when asked early:

```text
I would like to understand the role scope, level, and overall package first so I can give a thoughtful number. Based on senior .NET/full-stack roles with Azure/backend ownership, I am generally targeting a market-aligned total compensation range around {range}, depending on role level, benefits, and overall fit.
```

If you need to avoid a number:

```text
I am flexible for the right role and would prefer to understand the level and compensation band your team has budgeted. Could you share the expected range for this position?
```

## When Recruiter Shares A Range

```text
Thanks for sharing the range. That helps. Based on the role scope and my experience with .NET, React, Azure, backend APIs, and production systems, I would likely be looking toward the upper part of the range if the responsibilities align with senior ownership.
```

## When Offer Is Received

```text
Thank you for the offer. I am excited about the role and appreciate the team's time throughout the process. I would like to review the full written details, including base, bonus, equity or other components, benefits, role level, and start date. Can I take some time to review and come back with questions?
```

## Counteroffer Script

```text
Thank you again for the offer. I am genuinely interested in the role, especially because of {specific role/team reason}.

After reviewing the scope and market data for senior .NET/Azure/full-stack roles, I was hoping we could get closer to {target}. Is there flexibility in base salary, joining bonus, or total compensation to bridge the gap?
```

## Competing Offer Script

```text
I wanted to be transparent that I am also in process with another company and have an offer around {range/details if comfortable}. I remain very interested in {Company} because of {specific reason}. Is there room to improve the total package or timeline so I can make a well-informed decision?
```

## Asking For Time

```text
Thank you. This is an important decision, and I want to review the offer carefully. Could I get until {date} to respond? I will come back sooner if possible.
```

## Declining Politely

```text
Thank you for the offer and for the time the team invested in the process. After careful consideration, I have decided to move forward with another opportunity that is a closer fit for my current goals.

I enjoyed learning about the team and appreciate the opportunity. I hope we can stay connected.
```

## Role Quality Questions

Ask before accepting:

1. What does success look like in the first 90 days?
2. What level is this role mapped to internally?
3. How much architecture ownership is expected?
4. How hands-on is the role?
5. What is the team's current stack?
6. How does the team handle incidents?
7. What is the deployment process?
8. How does the team use Azure/cloud services?
9. How are code quality and technical debt handled?
10. How does the manager support senior engineers?

## Red Flags

| Red Flag | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- |
| vague role level | future expectations may mismatch |
| no clear manager | support and evaluation unclear |
| no production ownership clarity | senior role may be chaotic or shallow |
| pressure to accept immediately | poor candidate experience |
| compensation details not written | avoid misunderstanding |
| role far from target stack | weak long-term fit |
| no room for technical questions | culture concern |

## Negotiation Mistakes To Avoid

* Giving a low number before understanding scope.
* Accepting verbally before reviewing written offer.
* Negotiating aggressively without evidence.
* Ignoring total compensation.
* Ignoring manager and role quality.
* Making ultimatums unless prepared to walk away.
* Comparing offers without considering level and growth.
